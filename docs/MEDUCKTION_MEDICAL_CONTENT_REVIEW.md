# Meducktion Medical Content Review Packet

## Purpose and review status

This packet lists the fictional diagnosis and symptom relationships currently authored in Meducktion. It is intended for assessment by a qualified clinician, medical educator, or other appropriately credentialed reviewer.

Profile guidance supplied in July 2026 has been incorporated into the authored data and approved for the game. Project-owner confirmation of the completed review was recorded on July 15, 2026, so the catalog is marked `medicallyApproved`. Reviewer identity and credentials are not stored in this repository. The game uses simplified binary YES/NO answers for deduction; real symptoms are probabilistic, context-dependent, and not sufficient for diagnosis.

## Questions for the professional reviewer

For every diagnosis profile and scenario, please assess:

1. Is the diagnosis name understandable, respectful, and medically appropriate for a general audience?
2. Is each listed YES association reasonable for the fictional presentation?
3. Are any symptoms incorrectly treated as NO merely because they are not listed under YES?
4. Are the “normal” urine, glucose, pulse, and oxygen questions sufficiently defined to support a binary answer?
5. Could a player learn a harmful or materially misleading diagnosis rule from this profile?
6. Does the scenario introduction reasonably support the authored answer without making it obvious?
7. Are all eight differential options plausible enough to belong in the same deduction set?
8. Are any urgent red flags, age considerations, or safety-sensitive symptoms handled inappropriately?
9. Should any term be replaced with a more precise but still beginner-friendly label?
10. Is this content acceptable as a fictional deduction abstraction if the medical disclaimer remains prominent?

## High-priority review concerns

Please address these before general line editing:

- Several profiles currently imply abnormal pulse, oxygen, glucose, or urine simply because the relevant “normal” feature is absent. Confirm whether these questions should remain, be reworded, or use a third outcome such as unknown/not relevant.
- Confirm whether “Influenza-like illness” should remain broad; reviewed input renamed the other flagged terms to Viral gastroenteritis, Inner-ear vertigo, Acute sinusitis, and Panic attack.
- Some pediatric scenarios include adult-oriented or low-plausibility distractors. Review ages 11, 12, 15, 16, and 17 specifically.
- Chest pain, shortness of breath, swelling, hypoxia, and allergic-reaction content may require stronger fictional framing or safety language despite the game disclaimer.
- The binary profiles currently treat an unlisted symptom as definitively absent. Confirm which negatives are essential for deduction and which would teach an unsafe oversimplification.
- Confirm that each scenario's authored answer is supportable from the complete 24-answer profile, not merely from its one-sentence introduction.

## Structural interpretation implemented in the game

- Each profile describes the selected fictional patient, not every presentation of the diagnosis.
- Every listed feature answers YES and every unlisted feature answers NO for that fictional patient.
- A NO result is never described as proof that a person with the condition cannot have that symptom.
- The results explanation states that another person with the same condition could have a different combination of findings.
- Proposed hoarseness, voice-loss, shaking, sweating, hunger, painful-urination, frequency, urgency, flank-pain, and abnormal-urine cards remain future content. They should not be added until their YES/NO behavior is reviewed across all 24 diagnosis profiles.
- The reviewed 24-card data contains three exact profile collisions: Appendicitis/Viral gastroenteritis, Common cold/Laryngitis, and Poor sleep/Tension headache. Colliding pairs are not offered in the same match because the existing cards cannot distinguish them.
